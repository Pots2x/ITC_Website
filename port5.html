Portfolio 5 - Comparative Study On Different Types Of Motherboards

Motherboards are essential for computer systems as they are the center of a computer's circuit. They function as the connection between all the other parts of a computer, this is how every other computer part can function and communicate with each other. We may consider it as the platform for all computer parts to connect to for communication. How a motherboard is configured and developed if these components work together and how.

The motherboard is made up of a few components that provide functionality and use:

Processor

This is usually a CPU, it is the largest chip in the motherboard. A fan may be placed near it along with a heatsink to control its temperature.

Memory

In the form of RAM (Random Access Memory), there would be slots where the RAM would be placed, how many and what type of socket depends on the motherboard.

Expansion Slots

Depending on the motherboard, the number of expansion slots would differ. These slots allow the motherboard to take in more components and add more functionality.

Power Connector

This is where the motherboard would take in power from a power supply. This supplies power to the components connected to the motherboard.

BIOS

The BIOS (Basic Input/Output System) chip is where the low-level programs and code are stored. This can be considered firmware and is how your computer hardware operates.

CMOS battery and chip

The CMOS chip stores important information used by the computer, such as time, date, and hardware settings. The battery prevents such information from being erased when a computer is turned off or restarted.

Motherboards also have different forms, this determines their size and compatibility with different computers or computer components:

PCI (Peripheral Components Interconnect) refers to the amount of functional components that a motherboard can take in

SATA (Serial Advanced Technology Attachment) refers to the number of storage devices the motherboard can take in
