Portfolio 6 - Comparative Study on Different Types of Computers

Supercomputer

A very powerful computing machine that can reach up to PentaFLOPS of computational power, usually very large, and makes use of multiple computers (CPUs maybe also GPUs) to break apart tasks in a process called parallel processing.

Uses: Scientific Research, Advanced AI

Mainframe Computers

Typically weaker than a supercomputer but can still process large amounts of data, they can handle numerous calculations as they also make use of parallel processing. You may think of mainframe computers being a weaker supercomputer as they are typically used in businesses or situations that handle large data without it being extremely complex. Think of mainframes being used to handle the transaction load of an entire bank or two and a supercomputer handling the complicated computations of quantum physics.

Uses: Transaction Processing, Banking, Stock Exchanges

Minicomputers

Minicomputers were very popular in the 20th century, though they were weaker than mainframes they still provided ample computational power for large enough datasets. Today, they are considered obsolete.

Uses: Data management, banking, Transaction processing

Server

They are widely used for communications and handling requests and responses to their clients. They may also work on databases depending on the purpose of their construction

Uses: Networks, Databases

Workstations

These are computers that are designed to be used by professionals, thus they would require high-end hardware. Though, workstations are nowhere near as powerful as mainframes or supercomputers. Workstations are often created with a specific work in mind, either CAD software or maybe something that makes use of 3D software.

Uses: Engineering, Architecture, Animations

Microcomputer

This is usually used for everyday and personal use, thus this is the most common and commercialized type. Think of your personal computers or laptops. Though they are similar to workstations, they differ in function and purpose (which dictates their speed and power).

Uses: Personal use and Everyday use
