Portfolio 2 - Data, Information, and Information Systems

Data and Information

Although often confused with each other, as both are commonly used interchangeably, data and information have noticeable differences that greatly differ from each other. Simply put, data is any symbol, image, number, or sound that has no meaning as they are out of context or seemingly unrelated. Information then is the refinement of this data, where the data is given context to be comprehensive. Data is processed into information by a human, as humans contextualize the data with computers providing computational power. Once information is generated from data, this information is then comprehended into knowledge by the human. This knowledge is stored in the human, however, this knowledge can be returned into data as it can be externalized into information and stored into a media (storage media like video, hard drive memory, print, etc.) as data.

Information System

Information Systems (IS) use computer processes and technological solutions in management. It started in World War II and early into the 1960s when the TELEX machine allowed long-range communication between different organizational branches where IS became more and more mainstream (but usually in accounting departments). One prominent work of IS is the World Wide Web itself, as it is a type of IS called the Networked Information System (NIS). The goal of IS is to connect five elements: the people/users, processes, information resources, computing devices, and the communications infrastructure.

Different Types of Support Systems in Information Systems

There are six Major IS types: Transaction Processing System, Knowledge Work System, Office Automation System, Decision Support System, Management Information System, and Executive Support System. These can be divided into either the Operations Support System or the Managerial Support System. The Operation Support System is used by low-level managerial positions with routine decisions. These are tailored by the organization for the varied requirements of each job position as well as managerial positions.

The Knowledge Work System is designed to allow connections with experts in order to share and record their knowledge. This enables a constant update of information for the operations.

Office Automation System is the integration of telecommunications and technology to better the productivity and efficiency of the business functions of an organization.

The Decision Support System is a knowledge-based system used by senior managers to simulate the effects of their decisions, especially in situations wherein no predefined procedures are available.

Executive Support Systems gives top executives vital information needed for them to make strategically viable decisions. It takes sources from internally and externally, utilizing data and information gathered from other systems in the organization.
